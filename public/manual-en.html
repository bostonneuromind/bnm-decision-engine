1. What is Decision Catcher?

Decision Catcher is a clinical decision support system developed by Boston Neuromind LLC. It helps clinicians better understand your cognitive and emotional state, and recommends suitable training methods.

What the system does

  • Analyzes possible diagnoses from your symptoms (differential diagnosis)
  • Decomposes contributions when multiple conditions co-occur (comorbidity)
  • Automatically recommends personalized training cards
  • Measures outcomes after 4 weeks and re-evaluates diagnoses when needed
Important: Decision Catcher does not replace your clinician's diagnosis. All final decisions are made by your clinician. The system is a support tool.

2. Beta Program Enrollment

  1. Complete the beta signup form — Enter basic info (name, email, date of birth)
  2. Sign the consent form — Read and check 4 items, then sign
  3. (If minor) Parent consent — If parent is present, sign immediately; otherwise consent form is emailed
  4. Initial assessment (60-90 min) — First assessment with your clinician
  5. Weekly progress entry — 5-10 minutes each week to track changes
  6. 4-week outcome assessment (60 min) — Measure changes and review diagnosis

Total duration: ~8 weeks (4-8 weeks training + 4 weeks outcome tracking)

3. How the Assessment Works

During the initial assessment

  1. Identify your 5 most challenging current symptoms
  2. 5-axis evaluation: attention, executive function, emotional, social, somatic
  3. Enter temporal information about when symptoms started
  4. (Optional) Biometric measurements — HRV, CPT (continuous performance test)

How to prepare

  • Reflect on your state over the past week
  • List any current medications
  • Summarize past diagnoses or treatment history
  • Dress comfortably
Tip: The assessment is a conversation. Don't try to give "right" answers — just describe your state honestly. That leads to the most accurate results.

4. Understanding Your Results

What you'll receive

  • Clinical scores — Across 5 axes (attention/executive/emotional/social/somatic)
  • Differential diagnosis — System-estimated possibilities (e.g., ADHD 60%, GAD 25%)
  • Training recommendations — Personalized training cards
  • Training schedule — 4-8 week protocol

How to interpret results

  1. System scores are reference data. Your clinician interprets them with you.
  2. A high score doesn't necessarily mean "bad" — it helps you understand your patterns.
  3. Multiple diagnoses may appear simultaneously. This is common; the system shows priorities.
  4. "Differential diagnosis" results are not medical diagnoses. Your clinician's final evaluation is the diagnosis.

5. Weekly Progress Entry

Once a week, take 5-10 minutes to record your changes.

What you'll enter

  • Symptom changes this week (1-10 scale)
  • Whether you completed training cards and their effects
  • Sleep, exercise, stress levels
  • Free-form notes (if any)
Why it matters: As weekly data accumulates, the system learns your change patterns and provides more accurate recommendations. Please don't skip — enter every week.

When to enter

Enter at any convenient time during the week. You'll receive a reminder email once a week.

6. 4-Week Outcome Assessment

4 weeks after starting training, you'll have an assessment to measure changes. Takes about 60 minutes.

What happens in this assessment

  • Same 5-axis measurements as initial assessment (calculates change)
  • Training effectiveness evaluation
  • Review system's automatic diagnostic re-routing
  • Plan the next phase

Possible outcomes

  1. Effective — Continue same training, possibly adjust intensity
  2. Partial effect — Modify training combination (e.g., add emotional regulation to attention)
  3. Limited effect — Review diagnosis, consider different approach

7. Privacy & Data Protection

Information stored

  • Basic info (name, email, date of birth)
  • Clinical assessment scores
  • Weekly progress entries
  • Signed consent forms

How it's protected

  • All data is encrypted per HIPAA standards
  • (Minors) Children under 13 receive additional COPPA protection
  • Your information is not shared with third parties
  • For research disclosure, all identifying information is removed

Right to withdraw

You may withdraw from the beta program at any time. Upon withdrawal:

  • All data is deleted within 30 days
  • No penalty for withdrawal
  • Request via support@neurocatchers.com

8. Crisis Response

⚠️ If you need immediate help
If any of the following apply, call 911 or 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) immediately:
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Possibility of harm to yourself or others
  • Severe emotional crisis
  • Changes in reality perception (hallucinations, etc.)

Non-emergency situations

When you need help but it's not urgent:

  • Contact your clinician
  • support@neurocatchers.com
  • Tell a trusted family member or friend
Mandated Reporter notice: Boston Neuromind clinicians are legally required to report:
  • Imminent risk of harm to self or others
  • Suspected abuse of children, elderly, or disabled persons

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Does this system replace a doctor's diagnosis?
No. Decision Catcher is a tool that supports clinician decisions. All final diagnoses and treatment decisions are made by your clinician.
Q. Is there a fee for beta participation?
The beta program itself is free. However, clinical assessment or training fees follow the clinic's policy.
Q. What if I don't like the results?
Results are for reference. You can interpret them with your clinician and pursue a different evaluation direction if needed.
Q. Can I share my results with friends or family?
Your results are yours. However, during the beta stage, system recommendations may not be perfect, so we recommend interpreting them with your clinician.
Q. Can minors (under 18) participate?
Yes. However, parental/legal guardian consent is required. If parents are present, they can sign immediately; otherwise, we email the consent form.
Q. What happens after the beta ends?
After beta and official launch, you can continue using the system. Beta participants receive priority notifications.
Q. How do I delete my data?
Email support@neurocatchers.com — all data will be deleted within 30 days.
